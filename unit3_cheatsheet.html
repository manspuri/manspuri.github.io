HTML5 Cheatsheet

Courtesy of Mansi Puri

HEADLINE TAGS

  • <h1>...</h1> The largest and most important of the headlines.
  • <h2>...</h2> The second largest and most important of the headlines.
  • <h3>...</h3> The third largest and most important of the headlines.
  • <h4>...</h4> The fourth largest and most important of the headlines.
  • <h5>...</h5> The fifth largest and most important of the headlines.
  • <h6>...</h6> The smallest and least important of the headlines.

CONTENT TAGS

  • <p>...</p> Paragraph tag.
  • <img src="image URL goes here" alt="image name"/> Put image link in the src attribute; alt attribute is optional.
  • <ul>...</ul> Unordered list tag; list tags go within this tag.
  • <ol>...</ol> Ordered list tag; list tags go within this tag.
  • <li>...</li> List tag; each listed item goes inside its own list tag.
  • <div>...</div> Divides your HTML into sections (footer, header, sidebar, etc.).

INLINE ELEMENT TAGS

  • <a href="URL goes here">Link Title</a> Link tag; creates a link to outside sites.
  • <strong>...</strong> Makes the text within this tag bold.
  • <em>...</em> Makes the text within this tag italicized.

STRUCTURAL TAGS

  • <header>...</header> Header tags can be used to section off headers of the webpage's content.
  • <nav>...</nav> Nav tags for site navigation.
  • <section>...</section> Denote major sections of site; cannot have sections inside one another!
  • <article>...</article> Use for pieces of content such as blog posts, news articles, or comments.
  • <aside>...</aside> Use these tags for secondary content, such as sidebars.
  • <footer>...</footer> Use for footer of your site which may contain info about site author, contact details, or copyright information.
