Learnin' About Thinking

I am almost equal parts Concrete Sequential, Abstract Sequential, and Abstract Random. After reading characteristics from this website, I pulled the ones which I thought best represented my learning habits.

    Concrete Sequential
  • likes: order, logical sequence, following directions
  • learns best when: they have a structured environment
  • difficulties: questions with no right or wrong answers, working in groups
    Abstract Sequential
  • likes: analyzing situations before making a decision or acting
  • learns best when: able to work alone, have access to experts or references
  • difficulties: too little time to deal with a subject thoroughly
    Abstract Random
  • likes: focusing on the issues at hand, listening to others
  • learns best when: able to maintain friendly relationships, in a personalized environment
  • difficulties: competition, working with dictatorial/authoritarian personalities, accepting even positive criticism

Thinkin' About Thinking

When learning new material, I tend to retreat and experiment on my own. I thought that this was the best way for me learn because there are no distractions, I could concentrate on my own needs, allocate time for concepts which needed the most attention, etc. However, after pairing a few times, I realize that I learn more efficiently when I work with others. This is because I am not in a position to dwell on a problem and overthink solutions and whether I am on the right track. As a pair, we usually just jump into the challenge and learn as we go. Working with others pushes me out of inaction. Pairing also affords me the opportunity to see how others approach a challenge and then use similar techniques on subsequent challenges.

Still, working in pairs and asking for input is not my natural tendency. It's not my go-to strategy when I feel stuck on a challenge. But maybe it should be. Instead of spending hours on a challenge running my mind in circles thinking about finishing the challenge, doing it right, and putting enough into it, I should just get another perspective from a peer. This may not always be the appropriate solution but it should be something that I do more of.
